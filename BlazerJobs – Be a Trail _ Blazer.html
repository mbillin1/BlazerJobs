Ways to keep your Resolutions

January 21, 2019January 21, 2019 Blazer JobsLeave a commentEdit "Ways to keep your Resolutions"

Welcome to 2019!

One hundred years ago we were outlawing alcohol during Prohibition, Woodrow Wilson won the Nobel Peace Prize and the Versailles Treaty was signed by the Allies including Germany. We’ve come a long way since then and we are still striving to be better. Once that first day of the yea hits, we are programmed to make some important life(style) changes that we feel will better our new year. Most of us renew an exercise program gone to the back burner or we may take up a new form of cooking. Whatever it may be, try not to be discouraged when you feel your efforts waning. Here are some tricks that may help to keep you on the right track and give you an exciting new you!

Be sure to define your goals to yourself –

The more detail you can put into your resolution, the more reasonable you become to your future self. You know your strengths and weaknesses and what keeps you motivated. Give yourself details to work from and it’ll be easier to outline when it comes time to planning out your attack.

Keep a record/measurement –

Be sure to track your progress somehow. Maybe a journal or a calendar of events. My household uses Google Calendar to make task lists and once we are finished, someone gets to check it off the list. “If you can measure it, you can change it” is a fundamental principal of psychology. These mile markers will help you track your progress and find where you seem to fall of every so often.

Make someone accountable for you –

This may seem counter intuitive when it’s your goal, but if someone is helping to keep track of your status, it keeps you accountable. Your friend will be there to ask, “Why didn’t you finish your set?” or “Did you really want to order another beer?” This gives the resolution more girth as there are now two people keeping tabs

Put it on the schedule –

Seeing the task and giving it a time slot in your day is very crucial. This makes it a priority for you instead of a “I’ll get to it when I can.” Seeing it on the schedule makes it a part of your day and once it’s been completed, you can feel the sense of fulfillment wash over you.

Commit to what you can –

Say you’re on a business trip and don’t have an hour between meetings to go for that daily run. Jump on a treadmill and job for 20 minutes; at least it’s putting effort towards your goal instead of giving up on it completely. You set these goals for a reason and making excuses or having an “all or nothing” sense will negate the validity of your commitment to the resolution.

Get back on the horse –

If you slipped for a bit too long, don’t be discouraged. Pick up right where you left off and keep going. Learn from your mistakes. What made you fail the first time? Is this avoidable? Grow from these setbacks, it’s only human to fail.

“A bruise is a lesson… and each lesson makes us better.” ― George R.R. Martin, A Game of Thrones

Turkey Day Fun!

November 7, 2018 Blazer JobsLeave a commentEdit "Turkey Day Fun!"

We are officially in the holiday marathon season! With family coming in town, the oven is always on and the guests increase by tenfold every year with new marriages and new babies. It can be a bit stressful to keep everyone entertained and organized, especially if you have a child with special needs. Since we work very closely with the special needs departments in schools nationwide, we decided to come up with some fun activities for children during the craziness that is Thanksgiving.

Turkey Feather Hunt

A great game to keep little one’s actively involved in their gross motor development. This game helps with their independent searching skills as well as a concentration on sorting and colors.

  1. Cut out a turkey shape complete with gobble and beak.
  2. Have different colored long feathers hidden throughout a room in the house
  3. Once all the feathers have been found, count and sort by color!
  4. Keep it fun by having the children hide the feathers from the adults as well

Turkey Feather Hunt Game Tutorial

Sensory Turkey (credit:speechrooomnews.com)

Create a turkey that has all sorts of different sensory items for kids to connect to, describe and learn from. This activity leaves a lot of variety open to the imagination and many of the sensory items can be things found around the house. Try to make each feather different from its neighbor with varied tactile, audible, and pungent senses to keep everyone guessing.

  1. Cut out a turkey shape complete with long and wide feathers
  2. Find objects around the house small enough to glue onto the turkey feathers
  3. Blindfold the guesser and let them fell out each feather then determine what they are feeling
  4. Have the guesser use adjectives to describe the items
  5. If your children are old enough, create an adjective list

Sensory Turkey Guessing Game

Coloring book Turkey Place mats

For the younger kids who need to be watched a bit more closely, have them color and create personal Turkey place mats for each guest sitting at the table. This will make them feel like they are contributing while giving them an activity to be fully creative with no restrictions. Hopefully this will last them until the turkey hits them and then they are ready for dreaming of sugar plum fairies!

Turkey feather counting

Let’s reuse the Turkey feather hunt idea and flip it!

Draw a few turkeys with no feathers but add a number to each one at the top. Have your child draw the correct number of feathers for each number; maybe even add color amounts (2 red, 3 blue, one orange) to make it a bit more challenging. Funny enough, these drawings can be used as your turkey place mats! Every one they get correct on the first time, you can reward with a small slice of turkey or pumpkin pie for your sweet tooth. This activity keeps them focused on the task at hand while also sharpening their counting and color skills.

Thanksgiving Matching Cards

Keep them guessing!

Print out cards with Thanksgiving themes (turkey, pumpkin, ham, apples, pilgrim, etc.) with two for each image. Lay out one set and have your children holding the second set. They must now match the each card in their set with the cards laid out. Have a name associated with each set and once they two have been matched, have your child tell you the word associated; very useful in language development. This game can also be used when trying to learn a new language!

 

Hopefully these little activities will keep your little ones busy during the meal prep and just awake enough to last through the delicious dessert. From our Blazerworks family to yours, HAPPY THANKSGIVING!

On the Weekend: Waukegan, IL

October 31, 2018 Blazer JobsLeave a commentEdit "On the Weekend: Waukegan, IL"

Waukegan comes from the Potawatomie  word for “small fort” which is a derivative of the meaning of its original French name “Rivière du Vieux Fort” given before the 1820s. IT is a blue collar town that used to be a major hub of industrial movement for its large brother Chicago which lies less than an hour south. The town is made up mostly of descendants from Southeastern Europe and Scandinavian immigrants, mainly Finland and Norway. Waukegan is our “On the Weekend” post for this week as we have many opportunities available in and around this area. Let’s dive into this shoreline city and see what it has to offer us!

Since it is so close to the shores of Lake Michigan, many residents here make a living off of the lake’s bounty of fish. Captain Tony of the Diamond Ghost Charter has been fishing these waters for over 25 years so he is the knowledge you need to reel in your next big catch. These are rich waters with Coho salmon early summer and then Chinook into the early fall; there is also an abundance of Steelhead and Rainbow Trout throughout the year. Their charters run from April until October and they even offer overnight fishing trips!

credit: amberjack
credit: Diamond Charters
credit: Diamond Charters

The legendary Genesee Theater is a majestic theater house that dates back to 1927. Here is some history from their website: “In 1926, A.L. Brumund, H.C. Burnett, and D.T. Webb purchased land, at the corner of Genesee and Clayton Streets, from the Y.M.C.A. for $130,000. Their wish was to create a community center for Waukegan focused on high quality entertainment, commercial and living spaces. A year and a half and $1 million later, the luxurious movie palace named the Genesee Theatre, opened to the public.” They have been the distinct theater house ever since and show weekly film series complete with adult beverages. Big names have traveled here for one night show and the most recent performer was Craig Ferguson who will take the stage on November 3rd. Even just coming here to tour the facility will leave one nostalgic and missing the earlier years of cinematic greatness.

The Waukegan Park district encompasses 49 neighborhoods along with 735 acres of land for sports recreation parks, golf courses and green trails for the residents of Waukegan. This park district has something for every one: a dog exercise park, BMX skate park and even a physical fitness course. Many social events are held here including the annual Turkey Trot and a cemetery tour through the old Oakwood graveyard that is being held on November 2nd. For more information: https://www.waukeganparks.org/calendar/?mc_id=348

For a fun family outing, the Waukegan Public Library puts on a weekly movie every Sunday through the end of November. Free popcorn is offered and the movies typically start around 1:30pm. The week of November 4th is Book of Life while the following week of November 11th is Mall Cop. A safe way to watch family friendly movies while also starting a love for the public library!

ArtWauk is a monthly celebration of all things ART. Every month there are new art exhibits and bands to listen to while you explore the creations of local Waukegan artists. This event runs all year long and the upcoming date is for November 17th.

If you’ve had a good amount of Waukegan life, then head on down to Chicago which lies just south. Take in a baseball game at Wrigley field or have a legendary deep dish pizza slice.

Spooky Cities of America

October 24, 2018October 24, 2018 Blazer JobsLeave a commentEdit "Spooky Cities of America"

In the spirit of Halloween (see what we did there), we are showcasing a few of our spookiest cities in the great United States. These cities have the creepiest ghost stories, the most haunted houses and some of the darkest histories in the the whole nation. But they are also some fantastic cities to explore on your next travel job adventure!

Salem, Massachusetts “Witch City”

The town of Salem is your typical quaint New England town complete with fisherman and clam chowder. However, the very early history of Salem is quite disturbing. It was founded in one of the original colonies where religious freedom was a very touchy subject. The Salem Witch Trials occurred from 1692 to 1693. In that span, over 200 people were deemed “witches” and over 20 were executed. It began when a reverend’s two young female relatives started to display signs of the paranormal: distorting their bodies, screaming, throwing things and uttering strange sounds. They blamed three women: a Caribbean-born slave, a homeless beggar and an elder. All of them women and none of them with a voice to protect them from these allegations. The women were tried and two confirmed their innocence. The Caribbean slave Tituba however claimed the Devil came to her and made her sign his book so she could practice his “magic.”

This led to a slew of allegations from villagers about others in their crossings who may have had the “Devils’ magic” rained down on them. These began to spiral out of control and soon the village had their first “witch” hanging. By May 1963, the town had executed 20 of their own before a magistrate stepped in and stopped the madness. The town now embodies their past with the Salem Psychic Fair & Witches’ Market every October.

Salem Psychic Fair & Witches’ Market

Savannah, Georgia “Midnight in the Garden of Good & Evil”

Savannah has a ton of history due to the fact that it is the oldest city in Georgia and a large seaport back in its day. The city has experienced gruesome deaths ranging from bloody battles to debilitating diseases. However, its charm and grit has proven steadfast over time to make it one of the most visited haunted places in the country!

As we mentioned before, Savannah has seen its fair share of deaths including the ones killed in battle during General Sherman’s march to the Sea and the Siege of Savannah in 1779. The yellow fever epidemics here also took the lives of thousands; some say they are still stranded in the after life, not able to let go of their terrible deaths. One can also learn about the harrowing murders that have accompanied the city’s dark past. Many of the haunted ghost tours here describe the 1959 murder of three sisters on Abercorn ST or the depicted tale of the young male prostitute, an unfortunate character in the book “Midnight in the Garden of Good and Evil”.

Savannah Ghost Tours

New Orleans, Louisiana “Where the dead refuse to rest…”

It’s not just that every night here feels like Halloween or that Voodoo shops line the streets like Starbucks; New Orleans really is an extraordinarily spooky city. New Orleans has been the background for many famous vampire and occult stores, most famously the Anne Rice novels of “Interview with the Vampire”. If you have the craving for gumbo, vampires, and ghosts then this is the city for you!

The city had to build its graves above ground to prevent the sea level tides from flooding them and the contents within. Many of the ghosts that haunt this area came from an array of varied deaths. One of the worst epidemics to hit a major city happened in the mid 1800s, very similar to that which happened in the coastal city of Savannah. Yellow Fever claimed over 40,000 people, with one out of ten people perishing. This disease was carried throughout the city by tiny vermin insects: mosquitoes. They had to have mass graves for the dead and would ring bells every week, asking people for their dead to be buried.

There is also the sinister tale of the LaLaurie mansion. A place where slaves were tortured and people were afraid to enter. Delphine LaLaurie did terrible things in this mansion and it was finally overcome by a violent mob, sick over her actions. Most haunted tour guides will pass by this place to tell the full story.

Haunted New Orleans

Haunted Tours New Orleans

Chicago, Illinois “Al Capone’s playground…”

Not that you should have known this already, but Chicago has a very bloody past. During its early reign as the Windy City, Chicago was the hunting grounds for many murderous villains and the stage of multiple massacres. The St. Valentine’s Day massacre involved rival gangs and seven men were murdered by a firing squad February 14th, 1929. These men were associated with the Chicago North Side Gang, rivals to the Italian Southside gang, led by the notorious Al Capone. The gang leader was linked to hundreds of murders during his reign in Chicago and led to his infamous reputation as “Scarface”.

Tragedy has struck Chicago numerous times including the Great Chicago Fire of 1871 to the pre-Titanic Eastland ship disaster. On July 24th 1915, the Eastland “Speed Queen of the Great Lakes” was about to make its maiden voyage when it suddenly rolled onto its side, killing 844 people. Some have claimed to hear screams and see bodies drowning in the river, only to vanish in seconds.

The Hancock Center, sight of many unexplained deaths, was the inspiration for the cursed tower in the original “Ghostbusters.”

Chicago Hauntings Tour

Halloween in Chicago

San Francisco, California “Killer Gold Rush City”

If you lived in San Francisco during the Gold Rush of the late 1800s, there’s a good chance you murdered someone, knew someone murdered or got murdered yourself for a chunk of gold. There were over 1200 homicides committed between 1849 to the mid 1850s. That’s a pretty staggering number but also factoring in that the population was slightly above 20, 000 residents and it puts that tally in a different perspective.

Of course, no one does murder better than a serial killer and San Francisco had a doozy. He was deemed the “Demon of the Belfry”. Theodore Durrant had a taste for young, middle class women and was tried for two murders at the turn of the 20th century. He was superintendent at a Sunday School; this is significant because the two women’s mutilated bodies were fond stuffed in the church cupboards and the belfry. His acts of violence have been compared to the insanity of Jack the Ripper.

A few notable places to visit when searching for the paranormal:

-The Winchester house| built by a crazed widow, she kept building on to the house with doors that were walls inside and stairs that led to nowhere. She claimed she needed to confuse the spirits inside.

-Alcatraz| built on a small island in the bay, Alcatraz used to be brimming with maximum security prisoners convicted of heinous crimes. Few tried to escape but none never made it out alive…

-Curran Theater| An old theater from the 1920s where three people were murdered including a ticket taker that people claim to see wandering in the lobby.

Remember to be safe and respectful this Halloween, you never know which ghouls may be out and about

Our Favorite Camping Hacks

October 10, 2018 Blazer JobsLeave a commentEdit "Our Favorite Camping Hacks"

Now that you’ve gotten settled into your new city, it’s time to explore!

Most of the places we send out candidates have that outdoors feel (Denver, Seattle, Twin Falls) so a lot of the draw for our candidates is the ability to get in touch with nature. One of the best ways to do this is of course CAMPING!

If done properly, this can be a relaxing experience. We feel that anyone can be a good camper regardless of your camping experience. We have put together a few camping tricks that will help to make your adventure a little less stressful and allow you to enjoy it fully.

Camping Hack #1: Bugs

They can truly make a camping trip into a nightmare. There are a few preventative measures one can take to keep them at bay.

  • Burn sage in your fire; this helps to repel mosquitoes.
  • Quaker Oats grits can help to ward off pesky ants during a picnic.
  • To keep ticks off you, take a spray bottle and add one part tea tree oil with two parts water then spray on your shoes & socks. If one does attach to you or a friend, add this item to your first aid list: cotton balls soaked in liquid soap. Press the cotton ball onto the tick for about twenty seconds and watch it fall off. Make sure to save it if you are worried about Lyme disease to have it tested later.

Camping Hack #2: Fire

The fire is the quintessential reason most of us like to camp. Something about being around the primordial life source gives us a sense of peace and tranquility. However building a fire is sometimes one of the most stressful things for some people. I’m here to tell you the hardest part is just the beginning.

  • Use chips, preferably something oily like Doritos, as a fire starter.
  • Or you can bring cotton balls/pads soaked in wax or petroleum jelly as your starters.
  • You can also put charcoal in a cardboard egg crate for a portable fire starter. Just place in the fire pit and light
  • You can also use dryer lint instead of coals, just add melted wax.

Don’t forget the matches! Make sure they are in a waterproof jar (mason jar). You can glue sandpaper on the inside of the cap for easy lighting. If you prefer mechanical lighters, wrap the outside with a few feet of duct tape for easy access if needed.

Camping Hack #3: Sleeping

The quiet outdoors may pose a problem for those used to the city sounds to lull them to sleep.

  • Make your sleeping area as comfortable as possible by laying down foam tiles under your sleeping bag.
  • Stuff a sleeping bag sack full of your clothes as a makeshift pillow.
  • If you are feeling a little chill, heat up some water and place in a water bottle then put it at your feet inside your sleeping bag.
  • You can also put dry, clean clothes at the bottom of your bag to wick away any moisture that may cause cold feet.
  • Remember to have a pair of SLEEP ONLY socks that will always be clean and dry.
  • If traveling with a partner, invest in a double sleeping bag for extra warmth.
  • Eye masks sprayed with lavender is a great remedy for restless minds; invest in ear plugs!

coolthings.com

Camping Hack #4: Cooking

Cooking while camping can be a really fun experience. One can get really creative with making some awesome meals in the wild outdoors. Here are a few of our favorite camp cooking tricks.

  • Crack eggs and put the yolks in a water bottle. They will be ready to scramble for breakfast.
  • Freeze jugs of water and place them in the cooler. They will keep all your perishables cold and then once they have thawed, you have drinking water.
  • Wrap coffee grounds in coffee filters and tie off with a string. Place them in hot water in the morning for instant coffee.
  • Have leftover chips that weren’t used as kindling? Use them in a cast iron skillet to make some yummy nachos.
  • Looking for something fun? Dip marshmallows in Bailey’s Irish Coffee and toast over the fire, yum
  • Use Tic-Tac boxes to store your spices.
  • Invest in reusable plastic wine cups for all your drinking needs.
  • Cooking steaks can be challenging. For a great alternative to marinade, place fresh rosemary stems over hot coals for a smoky rosemary flavor. If you are worried about the meat burning, wrap them in cabbage and place in tin foil over the fire.
  • Cook canned cinnamon buns in a hollowed out orange for a unique fresh spin for breakfast!
myrecipejourney.com

Prepping a lot of your meals at home will help once you are at the campsite and relieves the stress of preparing.

If you are in an area that has bears, remember to keep all your food items in a bear can or tied high up in a tree.

Camping Hack #5: First Aid

One of the first things rangers, camp enthusiasts and wilderness experts will teach you is to make sure you have First Aid knowledge before heading out.

  • Know these plants that can cause scathing itchiness: Poison sumac, Poison Oak and Poison Ivy. BE able to identify these plants while walking the trails.
  • If one does make contact with one of these plants, use deodorant sticks to ward off some of the itchiness.
  • Have a small first aid kit handy maybe in a small tin or waterproof container. It should contain: tweezers, alcohol pads, band-aids, gauze, antibiotic ointment, and the essential aspirin plus Pepto-Bismol tablets.
  • In the case of a deep wound, make a tourniquet out of a breathable material like part of a t-shirt and make sure to tie it tight but not too much that it cuts off blood supply.

 

Every camping experience allows us to learn a little bit more about how to make the next time even better. One of our colleagues went to Alaska to camp in Denali National Park recently and learned about proper fire maintenance as well as bear prevention.

The wilderness is there to wow us, but we need to make sure we take care of it as well. Don’t leave your trash out and put out fires once you are done. Try not to disturb the foliage and please keep your distance to any animal that may come close to your campsite; they may have become used to humans but that is not an open invitation to feed them or touch them.

Follow us on social media and tell us your favorite camping story! Happy Trails!

How to travel with your furry family

October 3, 2018September 27, 2018 Blazer JobsLeave a commentEdit "How to travel with your furry family"

Traveling across country to a new destination is an exciting adventure and one that some of us like to share with our furry companions. Since many of the traveling therapists/educators are doing temporary assignments, they are sometimes stumped on how to bring their pets to these new locations without a lot of hassle. We at BlazerJobs understand this and are all avid pet lovers. So we decided to put together this post to alleviate some of that worry and tell you the best way to bring your four legged family along for the ride!

Keeping them covered: 

This includes their insurance and veterinary bills which should always be readily available. Sometimes one’s car or renter’s insurance can tack on a pet insurance policy to keep you safe from any unexpected bills that may arise if the pet gets sick.

Also, keeping your pet’s vaccines and medications up to date is key to keeping things in check. In a new city, you can get more Heartguard/Frontier if you go for a quick check up at any veterinary clinic which will range between $20 – $50. There is also a mail-in service that will deliver your pet’s flea/tick preventatives to you directly. Depending on the environment you are being assigned to should also decide the type of flea/tick prevention you use as different states have different species of these parasites.

Check out the differences here: https://www.reviews.com/flea-treatment-for-dogs/

Keeping them safe:

While you are in a different state/city, it is impertinent that your dog/cat has a microchip implant. This will alleviate any worries you may have about if they get lost; someone can find them, have them scanned at the nearest veterinary clinic and call you from your updated information. Always make sure this contact information is updated for each new assignment. One of our recruiters worked in Canada for a summer and got her 7 year old Husky Mix micro-chipped just for this adventure. The dog was able to roam free all day and would come home for dinner. Knowing she had a microchip was well worth the small expense.

Finding the right temporary home:

If you are an avid traveler, you probably understand that having your own accommodations such as a RV or camper van is the easiest route when dealing with your pets. If you need a bit more in structure, you may have to rent out a short term apartment or AirBnB. The AirBnB may be a bit more accepting when it comes to the breed/size of your dog but a lot of apartment houses are strict about the types of breeds they will allow. Here is a list of “Aggressive Breeds” for many apartments.

One way you can avoid being banned is register your pet as a “non-aggressive” breed. This is only handy if you have an adorable mutt. If your dog is part Husky and part Labrador, you need to have the veterinarian register them as Lab mix and keep out the Husky part.

If you do not have this option, try to set up a pet interview with the manager and your beloved dog. Also bring along all of their vet records and any accomplishments they have achieved including training courses and Canine Good Citizen awards. This CGC class is a great idea for any traveling dog as it registers them with AKC as a “good dog.” Click here for more information about the Canine Good Citizen Class.

You may be asked to pay a large pet security deposit; this is standard. However, to avoid any unforeseen costs when you move out, take detailed pictures and notes of how the apartment looked when you arrived and give the manager extras with dates on them. This stops them from trying to charge you “extra” pet fees for any damage your pet did not incur.

Arriving early:

With any big move, it can be stressful on all parties involved. Your pet may need a few days to recover before you start working so try to spend some quality time with them. Find the nearest dog parks for Fido or lay out all of Fluffy’s favorite cat toys and blankets in the new place so they can feel secure. It would also be a great idea to find all the local veterinary clinics and pet stores so you know exactly where everything is if you need it.

We understand that your pets are a part of the family and we will provide as much help as we can to find you all the best housing!

On The Weekend: Grand Junction, CO

September 26, 2018 Blazer JobsLeave a commentEdit "On The Weekend: Grand Junction, CO"

When one thinks of Colorado, the experiences that come to mind are hiking mountains, experiencing breweries and visiting cities such as Denver or Boulder. All great choices, but Colorado can be more than this!

This month we are visiting the engaging city of Grand Junction and seeing all the authentic activities she has to offer.

 

A few facts of Grand Junction:

  • Top international visitors come to Grand Junction from the United Kingdom, Canada, Germany, China, Australia, and the Netherlands (courtesy of GJ Tourism)
  • Top domestic visitors come to Grand Junction from around Colorado (52%), Texas, Arizona, and California (courtesy of GJ Tourism)
  • Grand Junction is the largest city on Colorado’s Western Slope (courtesy of visitgrandjunction.com)
  • The name Grand Junction comes from the junction of the Colorado River and the Gunnison River (courtesy of visitgrandjunction.com)
  • By the late 1970s the first grape vines were planted and it is now home to more than 20 wineries (courtesy of visitgrandjunction.com)
  • Nearby Grand Mesa Plateau boasts 10,000ft above sea level and is the largest flat topped mountain in the world

Grand Junction is able to host a multitude of travelers all looking for something different from the landscape. Whether it is the outdoorsy and adventurous type or the laid back and relax type, this city has it all. We will go through all the various types of activities one can expect to find in this expansive Western city.

Adventure:

  • Rock Climbing & Hiking
  • Unique River & Lakes
  • Colorado National Monument or Grand Mesa by bike or horseback

For the Family:

  • Dinosaur Museum, Trails, and Digs
  • Little Book Cliffs Wild Horse Range

Leisure Activities:

  • Over 20 wineries to choose from, you are in Colorado Wine Country!
  • Breweries & Farmer Markets

This weekend activities:

Tour of the Moon – The Tour of the Moon began in the 1980’s Coors Classic and later in the cycling movie American Flyers. To this day, it is one of the ultimate recreational rides in the western United States. This day of cycling takes you in majestic desert scenery and expansive views of the Western slope of Colorado. https://www.tourofthemoon.com/faq/

Bike trails through the fall foliage –

The Audubon Section of the River Front Trails is 1.4 miles of the 30 mile flat, mostly paved trail and takes you alongside the Colorado River. Also a great trail for bird watching.

Glade Park, located near the Colorado National Monument has several deep pools of water originating from the Little Dolores River which have become major watering holes for locals.

Grand Mesa is ideal for photographers, the world’s largest flat-top mountain is a great location to see the aspen trees change their colors. Color Weekend at Powderhorn Mountain Resort, September 28-30.

Rim Rock Drive in Colorado National Monument, set against 20,000 acres of impressive sandstone canyons and plateaus rising above the Colorado River. The 23-mile drive is open to both motorists and cyclists with an abundance of panoramic views of the fall foliage.

Wineries – This area boasts 23 wineries and now is the time for the harvest season which runs end of September into the middle of October. What’s great about all these wineries is that a few of them are close enough to bike between. What a gorgeous way to see the Grand Junction scenery with amazing natural landscapes to accompany! Check the link here for more information about how to bike your way through Colorado Wine Country.

 

There are great places to explore all around Grand Junction. If you travel west, you can head to Moab, Utah and explore the Canyonlands National Park, an expansive array of desert structures rising over the red sand.

Also along the Utah border to the north is the Dinosaur National Monument which houses, you guessed it, DINOSAURS! This is a great adventure for the kid in you or to bring the kids to. They have a fossil wall dedicated to the massive amounts of fossils they have discovered there. Feels like the opening scene from Jurassic Park!
